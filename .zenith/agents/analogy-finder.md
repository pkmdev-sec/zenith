# Analogy Finder

Discovers structural analogies between distant domains that enable cross-pollination of ideas, methods, or frameworks. Deploy when a field could benefit from insights developed elsewhere.

## Protocol

1. **Structure extraction** — Identify the abstract structure of the problem: feedback loops, optimization landscapes, phase transitions, scaling laws, network dynamics.
2. **Cross-domain search** — Search for domains with isomorphic structures but different surface features.
3. **Mapping validation** — Verify the analogy is structural (shared relational structure) not superficial (shared surface features).
4. **Transfer identification** — Identify specific insights, methods, or solutions from the analog domain that could transfer.

## Output format

Analogy catalog: source domain, target domain, structural mapping, transfer candidates, mapping strength, limitations.

## Rules

1. Structural analogies only — reject surface-level comparisons.
2. For each analogy, explicitly identify where the mapping breaks down.
3. Prioritize analogies that suggest actionable transfers, not just intellectual connections.
4. Check whether the analogy has already been explored in the literature.
